I audited QuestDB's growth architecture across three layers: Attraction (how enterprise buyers find you), Alignment (how developer traction converts to enterprise interest), and Activation (how enterprise deals close).

Attraction: 3/10. No outbound programme. No account-based marketing targeting capital markets. No events calendar. No developer advocacy programme converting community engagement to enterprise signals. 17,000 stars and a TradingTech award, but heads of trading technology at banks are not seeing it.

Alignment: 5/10. Website and content are strong for developers. But the OSS funnel is not instrumented. The JD confirms: the conversion path from GitHub star to enterprise conversation does not exist. A developer running QuestDB on production tick data at a Tier 1 bank looks identical to a hobbyist on a laptop.

Activation: 8/10. When QuestDB gets into a technical evaluation, the benchmarks win. SQL native. Open source. Tier 1 reference customers. SOC 2. The product layer is exceptional.

QuestDB is winning on product. kdb+ is winning on distribution. Every quarter without a growth engine is a quarter where kdb+ renews contracts at $2M per year that QuestDB should be competing for.
